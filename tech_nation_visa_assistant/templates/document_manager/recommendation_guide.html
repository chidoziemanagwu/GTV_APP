Recommendation Letter Guide

Comprehensive guide for your potential recommenders

Recommended Letter Structure

Opening & Introduction

State your relationship with the applicant, how long you've known them, and in what capacity. Mention your own credentials and position.

Technical Excellence

Highlight specific technical achievements, innovations, and contributions to the digital technology sector.

Leadership & Impact

Describe leadership roles, team management, and measurable impact on projects or organizations.

Innovation & Recognition

Mention awards, patents, publications, speaking engagements, or other forms of recognition in the tech field.

Future Potential

Explain how the applicant will contribute to the UK's digital technology sector and their potential for future success.

Strong Conclusion

Provide a clear recommendation and offer to discuss further if needed. Include your contact information.

Suitable Recommenders

Senior Colleagues & Managers

Current or former supervisors who can speak to your technical skills and professional achievements.

Examples:

  • Engineering Manager or Technical Lead
  • CTO or VP of Engineering
  • Product Manager or Director
  • Senior Software Architect
Why suitable: They can provide detailed insights into your technical contributions, leadership abilities, and professional growth.

Industry Leaders & Experts

Recognized figures in the tech industry who know your work and can vouch for your expertise.

Examples:

  • Tech conference speakers or organizers
  • Open source project maintainers
  • Industry consultants or advisors
  • Startup founders or investors
Why suitable: Their industry reputation adds credibility and they can speak to your standing within the tech community.

Academic & Research Contacts

Professors, researchers, or academic collaborators who can speak to your technical expertise and research contributions.

Examples:

  • University professors or lecturers
  • Research supervisors or collaborators
  • PhD advisors or committee members
  • Conference paper co-authors
Why suitable: They can validate your technical depth, research capabilities, and contribution to advancing the field.

Clients & Business Partners

External stakeholders who have directly benefited from your technical work and can speak to its impact.

Examples:

  • Client company executives or CTOs
  • Business partners or collaborators
  • Consulting clients or stakeholders
  • Technology vendors or suppliers
Why suitable: They can demonstrate the real-world impact and commercial value of your technical contributions.

Key Requirements

  • Must be written on official letterhead
  • Include recommender's full contact details
  • Signed and dated by the recommender
  • Minimum 1-2 pages in length
  • Focus on technical achievements and impact
  • Include specific examples and metrics
  • Written by someone senior in the field
  • Address future potential and UK contribution

Pro Tips

Quality over quantity: 3 strong letters are better than 5 weak ones.

Provide context: Give your recommenders your CV and key achievements to reference.

Allow time: Give recommenders at least 2-3 weeks to write a thoughtful letter.
